I·The Pipeline

From idea to published short,
in five stations.

Every Blind short was produced through the same sequence. Each tool performs the one task it does best; the judgment between the stations, from the brief to the final cut, remained mine.

I ChatGPTWrite Scripts, hooks, captions, and copy: drafted, refined, and tested in minutes rather than days.
II Google GeminiVisualize Characters, frames, and thumbnails generated to a brief, with a look held consistent across an entire series.
III HailuoAnimate Still images set in motion: MiniMax’s video engine turns frames into cinematic clips, with camera movement (pans, zooms, tracking shots) directed by prompt.
IV SunoScore Original music and audio hooks: jingles, parody tracks, and complete “Beats by Blind” compositions written to the script.
V Figma + AdobeFinish Captions, timing, and final assembly: the human judgment that makes the machine’s work feel intentional. (Adobe Certified Professional in Illustrator and InDesign.)

The value is not in the tools themselves, but in a system that allows one person to produce at the pace of a team.

II·Proof · Short-form Video

Forty-six shorts in four months, produced alone, for an audience that distrusts advertising.

Teamblind · US Growth Intern · Aug–Nov 2025 · youtube.com/@BlindApp/shorts ↗

Blind is the anonymous network where more than nine million verified professionals discuss salaries, layoffs, and workplace realities. My charge was to make Blind’s official channels feel native to that audience: sharp, current, and worth sharing. Using the pipeline described on the previous page, I wrote, produced, and published forty-six YouTube Shorts in four months, roughly three each week, as a team of one.

I did not simply post videos; I built formats: Blind Meme (tech-culture sketches), Beats by Blind (original parody music via Suno), Blind Choice (interactive polls), Blind POV (workplace confessions). Four repeatable series meant that every trend had a template waiting.

Guided by the data

AI-newsjacking memes such as “When ChatGPT Is Down,” “How Prompt Injection Works,” and “AWS Outage? AI Has Your Back(?)” consistently rose to the top of the channel, and the editorial calendar shifted toward same-week AI news accordingly. Speed over perfection, measured weekly.

Students receive practice that never repeats: every question is generated with AI, then reviewed by a person before it is published. I am responsible for the entire surface: product, brand, copy, pricing, and growth.

Why it matters here: this is the Wordbricks thesis in miniature. Take a formidable AI capability and make it feel simple, trustworthy, and worth paying for, in the product and in the copy that sells it.
