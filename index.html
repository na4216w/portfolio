I·The Pipeline

From idea to published short,
in five stations.

Every Blind short was produced through the same sequence. Each tool performs the one task it does best; the judgment between the stations — the brief, the taste, the final cut — remained mine.

I ChatGPTWrite Scripts, hooks, captions, and copy — drafted, refined, and tested in minutes rather than days.
II Google GeminiVisualize Characters, frames, and thumbnails generated to a brief, with a look held consistent across an entire series.
III HailuoAnimate Still images set in motion: MiniMax’s video engine turns frames into cinematic clips, with camera movement — pans, zooms, tracking shots — directed by prompt.
IV SunoScore Original music and audio hooks — jingles, parody tracks, and complete “Beats by Blind” compositions written to the script.
V Figma + AdobeFinish Captions, timing, and final assembly — the human judgment that makes the machine’s work feel intentional. (Adobe Certified Professional in Illustrator and InDesign.)

The value is not in the tools themselves, but in a system that allows one person to produce at the pace of a team.

III·Proof · AI Product & Marketing

I then founded an AI product — and wrote every word that sells it.

Focus Studio — Founder · Feb 2026–present · focusonprep.com ↗ · live product

Focus on Prep is a subscription Digital SAT platform that I operate as a registered business (포커스 스튜디오). Students receive practice that never repeats: every question is generated with AI, then reviewed by a person before it is published. I am responsible for the entire surface — product, brand, copy, pricing, and growth.

  • The product itself — developed hands-on with Claude Code, from the adaptive practice engine to the subscription flow.
  • Every word on the site — the hero (“The SAT rewards focus”), ten feature stories, pricing copy, and a comparison table that positions the product directly against Kaplan, Princeton Review, and UWorld.
  • The AI content engine — generative AI produces the question bank at scale; a human review gate keeps it trustworthy. “Always new, always vetted” is not a slogan; it is the architecture.
  • The positioning — “The big names sell a fixed bank — ours keeps growing,” the comparison headline as it appears on the site.

Why it matters here: this is the Wordbricks thesis in miniature — taking a formidable AI capability and making it feel simple, trustworthy, and worth paying for; in the product, and in the copy that sells it.
